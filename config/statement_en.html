The Goal

The goal of the game is to create a line of adjacent volcanoes which connect tiles on opposite sides of the Pentakis Icosidodecahedron board (an 80-tile sphere-ish shape).

(Image: Tom Ruen, Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons)

  Rules

On your turn you may either:

  1. Place a level 1 volcano of your color on an empty tile
    -or-
  2. Grow one of your volcanoes by one level until it's level 4

Progression of play

  1. Player one's turn (Blue always goes first)
  2. Player two's turn (Orange always goes second)
  3. Growth (every volcano on the board grows one level, except dormant volcanoes at level 4)
  4. Player two's turn (Orange's turn)
  5. Player one's turn (Blue's turn)
  6. Growth

This cycle repeats until a player wins by successfully connecting a pair of antipodes (tiles directly opposite each other).


When a volcano advances to level 4 (via a player's turn or via a growth turn), it erupts with the following effects:

  • The level 4 volcano becomes dormant (it stops growing)
  • The tiles adjacent to the erupting volcano are affected in the following ways:
    • Friendly volcanoes grow one level (this could trigger another eruption)
    • Enemy volcanoes are destroyed and leave behind empty tiles
Victory Conditions
Win by building an unbroken chain of volcanoes from one side of the board to the other.

  Expert Rules

The board is a 2D representation of a 3D object, so tiles connect around the edged of the board (e.g., N40 connects to N21 and S17 connects to S18).


Since these connections are tedious to set up, they are provided to you in the initialization inputs.


Eruptions can trigger further eruptions. If an infinite chain of eruptions is detected then the game is considered a draw.


To help easily determine which tiles are opposite each other, they are named in pairs so that N20 is opposite S20 and S32 is opposite N32.

  Game Input

Input (first move only)

Line 1: numberOfTiles, the total number of tiles on the board

Next numberOfTiles lines: 4 space-separated values:

  • tileName, the name of the tile at the given index
  • neighbor1, the index of a neighboring tile
  • neighbor2, the index of a neighboring tile
  • neighbor3, the index of a neighboring tile

Input (each turn)

Line 1: currentPosition, a space-separated list of the volcano levels of all numberOfTiles tiles on the board, with positive values for your volcanoes and negative values for your opponent's volcanoes

Line 2: validMoves, a space-separated list of valid moves in the current position

Output

Line 1: move, the name of the tile to play on or RANDOM to play a random move

Constraints
numberOfTiles = 80

Bots must respond within 100 milliseconds.
